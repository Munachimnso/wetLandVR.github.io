Lousisiana Wet Lands

Caminada Wetland

The Caminada Headland in Louisiana has witnessed high shoreline retreat rates, historically around 40 feet per year. Between 2006 and 2011, migration rates surged, exceeding 80 feet per year near Bay Champagne and 110 feet per year in the Bayou Moreau area. This increase was linked to Hurricanes Katrina and Rita in 2005, with prolonged breaches exacerbated by Tropical Storm Fay and Hurricanes Gustav and Ike in 2008, leading to significant sediment loss from the headland.

Delta Wetland

The Delta wetland, located on the east side of the Mississippi River near a 1973 flood crevasse in Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana, has undergone a transition. Originally an organic, brackish-saline marsh in decline, it transformed into a deltaic environment dominated by fresh and intermediate marshes after the crevasse. GIS analysis shows decreased marsh loss and the emergence of new marshes, with river sediments contributing to shallower areas

BlueBonnet Wetland

The Bluebonnet Swamp Nature Center spans 103 acres, focusing on conservation, education, recreation, and tourism. Its 9500-square-foot building features live animal exhibits, photographic displays, and a notable waterfowl decoy carving collection. Gravel paths and boardwalks connect diverse habitats, including a cypress-tupelo swamp and hardwood forests. The center hosts abundant wildlife, with over a mile of trails offering views of various bird species, snakes, turtles, and mammals like raccoons, rabbits, opossums, armadillos, squirrels, foxes, coyotes, deer, and otters.
